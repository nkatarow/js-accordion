• Business Income and Extra Expense

    ➕

    An example of how this coverage protects you would be after a fire or natural disaster; your business is damaged and will be closed for several months. Business interruption coverage will cover lost profits during that period of time and additional expenses incurred as a result of the event.

  • Accounts receivable

    ➕

    An example of how this coverage protects you would be after a fire that damages your building and destroys your customer billing records. You have to hire a collection agency to pursue delinquent accounts and they discover that some customers planned to not pay their accounts. This coverage would pay for those additional expenses and all amounts due from your customers that you are unable to collect.

  • Business Personal Property

    ➕

    Examples of business personal property include computers, laptops, printers, furniture and office supplies you own. Additionally, if you rent space and have invested in tenant improvements, your policy provides protection for those investments.

  • Valuable papers and records

    ➕

    The importance and level of valuable papers coverage varies by company and industry. Architects, lawyers and doctors have a much greater exposure than a small retail business. Our policy includes a base level coverage, which can be increased to adequately protect businesses with larger values of valuable papers.

  • Deductible for property coverage

    ➕

    There is one deductible for property coverage and this represents what you will pay out of pocket for any property related claim.

  • Windstorm or Hail percentage deductible

Liability coverage

  • Damage to premises rented to you

    ➕

    If you rent space as part of your business operations, this coverage will pay up to the specified limit if you are responsible for damages to the premises you rent or occupy if the damage results from fire, lightning or explosion.

  • Business Liability

    ➕

    Legal defense costs associated with lawsuits can be expensive. General Liability covers these defense costs as well as any judgments for covered losses that your business is held liable for. Examples of covered losses include someone slips and falls on your premises or an individual or business alleges that you have damaged their property.

  • Medical expenses per person

    ➕

    Your business owners policy provides $5000 for reasonable medical expenses such as first aid, hospitals, nursing, ambulance and funeral expenses when persons are injured on your premises. Medical coverage pays regardless of your legal liability when injuries arise from a covered loss.
